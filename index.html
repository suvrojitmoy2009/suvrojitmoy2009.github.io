Jun 2026Agentic AI

What "agentic" actually means once you've shipped one

Everyone's calling everything an agent right now, and honestly it's making the term less useful. After building an AI feature inside our Dynamics 365 setup that cut sales rep workload by 30%, here's the line I now use: if it can only answer when asked, it's a chatbot. If it can decide what to do next, check its own work, and only loop you in when something's genuinely ambiguous, that's agentic. Most "AI agents" I see in demos are the first thing wearing the second thing's name.

May 2026GenAI in CRM

The boring part of GenAI is where the value actually is

Nobody gets excited writing a prompt that summarizes a support ticket. But that's the part that saves five hours a week, not the flashy demo. The lesson I keep relearning in CRM work: the unglamorous, repetitive task your team complains about quietly in standups is almost always the best candidate for automation — not the impressive-looking edge case everyone wants to showcase.

Apr 2026Data Pipelines

Why I trust automation more after building it badly once

My first Azure Logic Apps pipeline broke quietly for two days before anyone noticed the sync had failed. That was a useful kind of embarrassing. Now every pipeline I design has a "tell me when you're wrong" step built in from day one — alerts, validation checks, a human checkpoint somewhere. The goal of automation isn't removing people from the loop; it's making sure the loop only needs them when it actually matters.

Mar 2026Career

Going from BA to building AI features, without quitting the day job

I didn't pivot into AI — I just kept asking "could this step be smarter?" on every Power Automate flow I touched. That question turned a basic CRM cleanup task into an AI-assisted feature within a few sprints. You don't need a separate career track to start working with GenAI; you need a backlog of annoying manual steps and permission to experiment on one of them.
